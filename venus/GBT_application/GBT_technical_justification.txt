Technical Justification

Receiver Configuration

Two receivers are required, used on separate sessions. 

Sessions A1 and A2 (1299.5 MHz): L-band receiver (Rcvr1_2, 1.15 to 1.73 GHz), currently available.

Sessions B1 and B2 (2304 MHz): S-band receiver (Rcvr2_3, 1.73 to 2.6 GHz), currently available.

Backend: GBT data will be recorded using the JPL Radar backend (GBT Proposer's Guide, Section 4.3.7, p. 27), which is designed for planetary radar receive applications and provides wideband IQ recording with configurable frequency and time resolution. We have configured the backend with 1.0 kHz frequency resolution and 10 millisecond time resolution, providing sufficient spectral coverage to capture the Venus echo across the full expected Doppler shift range (plus or minus 85 kHz at 1299.5 MHz, plus or minus 150 kHz at 2304 MHz) while preserving the time resolution needed for offline coherent integration. The coherence time of 0.667 seconds at 1299.5 MHz and 0.376 seconds at 2304 MHz sets the maximum coherent integration interval. All data will be released in SigMF format under CC0 and/or GPL 3.0 immediately upon acquisition.

Link Budget

The bistatic link budget uses the monostatic radar range equation adapted for the bistatic case:

Pr = Pt x Gt x Gr x lambda^2 x sigma / (4 pi)^3 / R^4
where sigma = rho_eff x pi x R_venus^2, and rho_eff is the disk-integrated effective radar albedo computed from the Magellan GREDR reflectivity map for the specific sub-Earth longitude at each observation date.

Both configurations close under coherent integration alone with approximately 12.7 dB of margin above the 10 dB detection threshold. The L-band configuration benefits from lower Doppler spread at 1299.5 MHz (1.50 Hz vs 2.659 Hz), giving a longer coherence time and greater processing gain that offsets the lower antenna gain at that frequency. No non-coherent integration is required for detection with GBT receiving. Non-coherent integration is required of the smaller participating stations to close the link independently.

Note on S-band notch filter: According to the Proposal Writer's Guide, the GBT S-band receiver has a permanently installed superconducting notch filter nominally covering 2300-2360 MHz, installed to suppress Sirius XM satellite radio interference. Our proposed S-band transmit frequency of 2304 MHz falls within this nominal range. 2304 MHz is in the amateur primary allocation (2300-2310 MHz), below the actual Sirius XM satellite downlink band which begins at 2320 MHz. Analysis of published Breakthrough Listen GBT S-band data suggests the filter's lower rolloff edge may be near 2310 MHz rather than 2300 MHz, potentially placing 2304 MHz in the filter transition band rather than the stopband. We have contacted GBO staff through the help desk to characterize the filter attenuation specifically at 2304 MHz. If 2304 MHz proves unusable, the fallback frequency for S-band sessions is 2390 MHz, which is in the amateur primary allocation *above* the Sirius XM band, clear of the notch filter, and within the S-band receiver's tuning range. Coordination with DSES regarding frequency agility between 2304 MHz and 2390 MHz is underway. 

Observation Sessions and Time Request

Venus remains within 10 percent of minimum distance from approximately October 7 through November 12, 2026, with CNR in 1 Hz exceeding +13 dB throughout this period. Four sessions are proposed to provide scheduling flexibility, Venus surface longitude diversity, and dual-frequency coverage. We are asking for 2.5 hours within each of the following four data ranges. 

Session A1: October 19-20, 1299.5 MHz (L-band), 2.5 hours plus 30 minutes overhead, elevation approximately 32 degrees at Green Bank and approximately 20 degrees at Dwingeloo.

Session B1: October 22-23 (conjunction), 2304 MHz (S-band), 2.5 hours plus 30 minutes overhead, CNR in 1 Hz +15.54 dB (at conjunction distance), elevation 33 degrees at Green Bank and approximately 33 degrees at DSES. 

Session A2: October 25-26, 1299.5 MHz (L-band), 2.5 hours plus 30 minutes overhead, elevation 34 degrees at Green Bank and approximately 21 degrees at Dwingeloo.

Session B2: October 31 to November 1, 2304 MHz (S-band), 2.5 hours plus 30 minutes overhead, CNR in 1 Hz +15.29 dB (at session distance), elevation 37 degrees at Green Bank and approximately 35 degrees at DSES. 

Exact observation windows within each session date will be scheduled to maximize simultaneous visibility from participating transmitting stations. For example, sessions A1 and A2 require coordination with the Dwingeloo telescope (CAMRAS). B1 and B2 include DSES. Observation windows will be scheduled during periods of simultaneous Venus visibility from both sites on the session dates. Final scheduling coordination with transmitting stations will occur after proposal acceptance and is ORI's responsibility. GBO scheduling staff will be consulted (and heeded) during the observing script preparation phase. 

Venus is a solar system object. Coordinates given in the sources section are approximate at inferior conjunction. It is anticipated that GBT will track Venus using its solar system ephemeris during the four observation windows. 

Total time request: 4 sessions of 2.5 hours with 0.5 hours overhead = 12 hours.

Sessions A2 and B1 are separated by three days at nearly identical Venus distance and elevation, enabling direct frequency comparison with minimal geometric change between observations. Any two sessions constitute a scientifically complete result. The four-session structure is a scheduling convenience, not a scientific requirement.

Weather Category and Scheduling

Both L-band and S-band fall in the poor weather category (below 8 GHz), the least constrained weather category in GBO scheduling. This maximizes scheduling flexibility. Sessions need not be scheduled consecutively.

Round-Trip Timing

At inferior conjunction (approximately 40.9 million km), the one-way light travel time is approximately 136 seconds, giving a round-trip delay of approximately 272 seconds. GBT will record continuously for the full session window. Transmitting stations coordinate transmission schedules with ORI in advance. GBT requires no real-time knowledge of the transmission schedule. All timing analysis is performed offline using the recorded SigMF files and published ephemeris data.

Data Products and Volume

GBT will record raw IQ data in SigMF format. Data will be transferred to ORI within 48 hours of each session and released publicly under CC0 or equivalent open source license. Expected data volume is approximately 2 GB per session at 1 MHz sample rate over 2.5 hours using complex float32 samples. Total across all sessions: approximately 8 GB. ORI requests waiver of the standard proprietary period. All data will be released immediately.

RFI Considerations

The National Radio Quiet Zone provides exceptional protection at both target frequencies. 1299.5 MHz and 2304 MHz are amateur radio allocations. The CAMRAS March 2025 experiment confirmed adequate RFI environment at Dwingeloo at 1299.5 MHz. The NRQZ environment at Green Bank is substantially cleaner. No significant terrestrial RFI is anticipated at either frequency
