Technical Justification

Receiver Configuration

Two receivers are requested for this proposal. Both are prime-focus receivers, enabling relatively fast receiver changes between session types (~30 minutes nominal per MPIfR wiki).

Sessions A1 and A2 (1299.5 MHz): P217mm 7-Beam receiver (1260-1510 MHz), central horn. The central horn provides dual circular polarization (LCP/RCP) simultaneously, which matches Dwingeloo's RHCP transmit and allows simultaneous recovery of both opposite-circular (OC, expected quasi-specular echo) and same-circular (SC, depolarized roughness return) polarizations. With the EDD backend, the central horn is the operational configuration. We understand that the six outer horns are not required for this experiment.

Sessions B1 and B2 (2304 MHz): P170mm UBB receiver (1.3-6.0 GHz). The UBB is the only operational Effelsberg receiver that covers 2304 MHz. It provides two linear polarization channels. Linear-to-circular polarization synthesis in post-processing follows the methodology published for the March 2025 CAMRAS/Stockert experiment, where two linear channels were combined with a measured phase offset to yield circular polarization.

Backend: The Effelsberg Direct Digitisation (EDD) system is the preferred backend, with raw IQ recording at a sample rate sufficient to cover the expected Doppler-shifted return. At 2304 MHz the maximum expected Doppler shift is approximately plus or minus 150 kHz. At 1299.5 MHz approximately plus or minus 85 kHz. A 1 MHz recording bandwidth provides comfortable guard band at both frequencies. If direct IQ recording at 1 MHz bandwidth is not supported in the standard EDD configuration, we would welcome guidance from MPIfR on an appropriate alternative (wider bandwidth recording with offline decimation is fully acceptable).

IQ data will be recorded to disk in SigMF format for offline analysis and immediate public release. We request confirmation from Effelsberg staff of the exact center frequency, bandwidth, and recording format prior to the observation sessions. We are prepared to work with Effelsberg support staff on observing script preparation.

Link Budget

The bistatic link budget uses the monostatic radar range equation adapted for the bistatic case:

  Pr = Pt * Gt * Gr * lambda^2 * sigma / ((4*pi)^3 * R^4)

where sigma = rho_eff * pi * R_venus^2, and rho_eff is the disk-integrated effective radar albedo computed from the Magellan GREDR reflectivity map for the specific sub-Earth longitude at each observation date.

Assumed parameters (explicitly flagged for MPIfR confirmation):

Effelsberg aperture efficiency at 1299.5 MHz: 0.65 (assumed, conservative). Actual value comes from MPIfR.

Effelsberg aperture efficiency at 2304 MHz: 0.60 (assumed, conservative for UBB wideband feed at mid-band). Actual value comes from MPIfR.

System temperature Tsys at 1299.5 MHz (P217mm central horn): 30 K assumed (working value, within the range expected for a modern cooled L-band receiver on a 100-m dish). Actual value to be corrected/confirmed by MPIfR.

System temperature Tsys at 2304 MHz (P170mm UBB): 35 K assumed. Published lab measurements give ~25 K; on-sky measurements with the post-filter UBB at mid-band should be closer to lab value. Actual value to be corrected/confirmed by MPIfR at the requested frequency.

TX and RX line losses: 0.5 dB each (1.0 dB total, assumed typical).

The link budget margin scales directly with Tsys. The table below shows example CNRs in 1 Hz at minimum Venus distance (40.93 million km, October 22, 2026) for a range of Tsys values, so MPIfR can see what we are seeing.

Observation Sessions and Time Request

Venus remains within 10 percent of minimum distance from approximately October 7 through November 12, 2026, with CNR in 1 Hz exceeding +13 dB at Effelsberg throughout this period (for Configuration 1 at nominal Tsys). Four sessions are proposed to provide scheduling flexibility, Venus surface longitude diversity, and dual-frequency coverage. We request 2.5 hours for each session plus 30 minutes of overhead.

  Session A1: October 19-20, 1299.5 MHz (L-band, P217mm), 2.5 hours + 0.5
  hours overhead. Venus elevation at Effelsberg approximately 23 degrees
  at upper culmination.

  Session B1: October 22-23 (inferior conjunction), 2304 MHz (S-band, UBB),
  2.5 hours + 0.5 hours overhead. Venus elevation at Effelsberg
  approximately 24 degrees at upper culmination.

  Session A2: October 25-26, 1299.5 MHz (L-band, P217mm), 2.5 hours + 0.5
  hours overhead. Venus elevation at Effelsberg approximately 25 degrees.

  Session B2: October 31 to November 1, 2304 MHz (S-band, UBB), 2.5 hours
  + 0.5 hours overhead. Venus elevation at Effelsberg approximately 28
  degrees.

The four session dates above match the date range of ORI's parallel GBT proposal for the same campaign, so that scientific results across receive stations can be compared on identical Venus surface geometry. However, the UTC hours within each date will be selected for European station visibility (Dwingeloo and Bochum to Effelsberg) rather than for the GBT observation hours. Longitude separation between the European and North American stations means the Effelsberg observation time within a given date will typically differ from the GBT observation time by several hours. The date range is aligned; the hours within each date are picked independently for each receive site based on its mutual visibility with the relevant transmitter.

Sessions A1 and A2 require coordination with the Dwingeloo telescope (CAMRAS). Sessions B1 and B2 require coordination with one or more 2304 MHz transmitting stations. Observation windows will be scheduled during periods of simultaneous Venus visibility. Final scheduling coordination with transmitting stations is ORI's responsibility and will occur after proposal acceptance. Effelsberg scheduling staff will be consulted during the observing script preparation phase.

Venus is a solar system object. Coordinates given in the sources section are approximate at inferior conjunction. It is anticipated that Effelsberg will track Venus using its solar system ephemeris during the observation windows.

Total time request: 4 sessions of 2.5 hours with 0.5 hours overhead each = 12 hours.

Sessions A2 and B1 are separated by three days at nearly identical Venus distance and elevation, enabling direct frequency comparison with minimal geometric change between observations. Any two sessions constitute a scientifically complete result. The four-session structure is a scheduling convenience, not a scientific requirement. If Program Committee allocation is constrained, we would prioritize in the following order:

  1. Session A1 (1299.5 MHz, Dwingeloo confirmed transmitter)
  2. Session B1 (2304 MHz, near conjunction, maximum signal)
  3. Session A2 (1299.5 MHz, second L-band opportunity)
  4. Session B2 (2304 MHz, extended baseline)

Dependency on 2304 MHz Transmitter

Sessions B1 and B2 depend on confirmation of a 2304 MHz transmitting station. AMSAT-DL Bochum is the primary candidate. If no qualified transmitter confirms by September 1, 2026, ORI will inform MPIfR and withdraw the S-band session requests. Withdrawal of B1 and B2 has no impact on the primary science objective (L-band detection at Configuration 1).

Round-Trip Timing

At inferior conjunction (approximately 40.9 million km), the one-way light travel time is approximately 136 seconds, giving a round-trip delay of approximately 272 seconds. Effelsberg will record continuously for the full session window. Transmitting stations coordinate transmission schedules with ORI in advance. Effelsberg requires no real-time knowledge of the transmission schedule. All timing analysis is performed offline using the recorded SigMF files and published ephemeris data.

Data Products and Volume

Effelsberg will record raw IQ data in SigMF format or equivalent. Data will be transferred to ORI within 48 hours of each session and released publicly under CC0 or equivalent open source license. Expected data volume is approximately 2 GB per session at 1 MHz sample rate over 2.5 hours using complex float32 samples. Total across all four sessions: approximately 8 GB. ORI requests waiver of any standard proprietary period. All data will be released immediately upon reduction.

RFI Considerations

The Effelsberg site's natural valley shielding provides substantial RFI protection at both target frequencies. 1299.5 MHz and 2304 MHz are amateur radio allocations in Region 1 (Europe). The CAMRAS March 2025 experiment confirmed adequate RFI environment at Dwingeloo at 1299.5 MHz. The 2304 MHz band is below the 2.4 GHz ISM allocation and should benefit from Effelsberg's valley shielding relative to urban sites. We are not aware of known RFI sources at either frequency that would compromise the measurement, but we welcome guidance from MPIfR on their current RFI environment at both target frequencies.
