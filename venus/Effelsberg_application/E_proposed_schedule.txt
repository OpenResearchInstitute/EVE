EVE 2026
Effelsberg-Centered Session Schedule
Open Research Institute
Last updated: 2026-04-20

This schedule is centered on Effelsberg as the primary receive station. It is complementary to the GBT-centered schedule (GBT_session_schedule.txt). Volunteer transmitting stations (Dwingeloo, DSES, Bochum) are encouraged to target both schedules where their equipment and availability permit.

S-band sessions (B1, B2) are designated as open slots for any European
station with 2304 MHz transmit capability. Dwingeloo has confirmed a feed focus problem at 2304 MHz and cannot transmit at this frequency.
Bochum may be able to transmit at 2304 MHz pending equipment funding.
Other European stations are welcome to participate.

NOTE: Effelsberg participation is pending confirmation from MPIfR.
All Effelsberg parameters are marked #VERIFY in the link budget notebook.

Contact: Uwe Bach, MPIfR (email sent, guidance given, proposal sent).

======================================================================
Date:                       October 19-20, 2026
Effelsberg recording block: 10:51–13:51 UTC (3 hours including overhead)
Science time:               2.5 hours
Overhead:                   30 minutes
Effelsberg receiver:        S45mm or equivalent L-band feed (#VERIFY with MPIfR)
Effelsberg elevation:       approximately 20 degrees at peak

Transmitting station:       Dwingeloo 25m (CAMRAS), 1299.5 MHz, 1000W
Dwingeloo+Effelsberg window: 09:46–14:26 UTC (4.7 hours)
Dwingeloo elevation:        approximately 20 degrees at peak
Echo arrival at Effelsberg: 13:32–14:28 UTC (Dwingeloo window + 136s round trip)

Note: Effelsberg and Dwingeloo are ~350 km apart and have nearly identical Venus visibility windows. Recording block centered on shared window. Effelsberg records independently. No real-time coordination required.

GBT companion window (if accepted): 12:28–15:28 UTC
Overlap with Effelsberg block: 12:28–13:51 UTC (~1.4 hours)
Two independent 100m dishes recording simultaneously during overlap.
